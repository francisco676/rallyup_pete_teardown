part 1

pete caputa's voice fingerprint.

the 9-section structured output we produce for every exec we onboard. every claim below is backed by a verbatim line from his post archive. no invented examples.

1. core identity

peter caputa iv is the ceo of databox, an ai-powered analytics platform serving 20,000+ businesses and 1,200+ professional services firms. before databox he spent 9 years as the founder and leader of the hubspot solutions partner program, where he helped thousands of agencies scale to multi-million-dollar exits. based in boston, ~20 years building b2b revenue systems. his linkedin reads as a ceo-as-practitioner feed: he writes as someone actively running the plays he's describing, not commenting from above.

2. voice archetype

the pragmatic contrarian.

he takes the conventional playbook, names what it costs, and hands the reader a scrappier version of it. he's earned the right to disagree publicly because he's run both sides (hubspot enterprise and databox startup), and his critiques always come with 3-5 numbered alternatives. he's not a dunker. he's an operator who thinks cutting-edge advice doesn't fit 99% of the people reading it.

3. style fingerprint

3a. sentence mechanics

~15
avg words / sentence
mixed
length variance
5/10
fragment frequency
  • pov: first-person singular dominant. switches to "we/our" for databox team. switches to "you" when giving advice.
  • verb patterns: active voice. present tense for claims. past tense for anecdote setup.

verbatim rhythm samples from the corpus:

"this is the exact advice i used to give in 2010. publish more blog posts to get more search traffic. the problem? it doesn't work anymore."
"you shouldn't need a computer science or advanced mathematics degree to get insights out of your data."

3b. paragraph rhythm

  • avg 3-6 sentences per paragraph (denser than most linkedin writers)
  • natural paragraph breaks, not aggressive one-per-line
  • numbered emoji (1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣) or arrow bullets (➡️) break up the prose
  • visual rhythm: "prose-then-payoff". 2-3 paragraph argument followed by an enumerated action list

3c. opening patterns (5 distinct)

  1. specific-anecdote opener. grounds the post in a real conversation. "the other day, an agency owner told me they were going to ramp up their content production..."
  2. direct-quote opener. leads with a quoted message. "'hey peter, i wanted to follow up with another email because i didn't hear back from you...'"
  3. bold data claim. specific metric upfront. "in the last 3 months, my linkedin posts have racked up 70% more impressions..."
  4. contrarian declaration. states the counterintuitive with no setup. "'build a media company' is such crazy advice for a small business."
  5. product value proposition. opens with what the product enables. "with the launch of databox analytics, i believe we've developed the easiest way to monitor, benchmark, predict..."

3d. closing patterns

  • single-sentence imperative cta. "leave a comment and connect with me if you want to come."
  • summative principle. "think like a media company. just don't be in the business of financing media production."
  • bold wager. "i'll wager that it'll be 10x better than writing 48 blog posts."
  • future tease. "stay tuned for a boatload of announcements."
  • ps wink. "ps. notice i didn't say generative ai once, but here you go."

3e. connectors & transitions

real pivot phrases from the corpus:

  • "the problem?" fragment as pivot. his signature move.
  • "so, how can you..." pivot to the solution section.
  • "that's especially true if..." narrowing a claim.
  • "that's when he suggested..." narrative pivot.
  • "here's 3 things..." list intro.
  • "want some actionable advice?" rhetorical pivot to payoff.
  • "all that said..." concession before the turn.
  • "the truth is..." introspective pivot.

3f. signature punctuation & formatting

  • em-dash / double-hyphen (--). used frequently. comfortable with dashes as interruption.
  • ampersand (&) substitutes for "and" in list-like contexts.
  • "really, really, really" triple. signature intensifier.
  • numbered emoji 1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣. appears in nearly every actionable-advice post as the payoff structure.
  • arrow emoji ➡️ and →. for sub-lists and enumeration.
  • parenthetical asides. frequent, for caveats and clarifications.
  • all-caps for emphasis. rare but present: "ANY credit", "NOT".
  • ps / p.s. appears in product launches as a wink.
  • hashtags. sparse, occasionally 2-4 per post in older posts.
  • ellipsis "..." used at list ends and for trailing thoughts.

3g. anti-slop overrides

patterns that would be banned for most writers but are signature for pete.

  • em-dashes. OVERRIDE. they're part of his style.
  • "really, really, really" triple. PARTIAL. allowed as intensifier.
  • rhetorical question-then-answer fragment. PARTIAL. "the problem? it doesn't work anymore." is rhythmic signature.
  • numbered emoji lists. OVERRIDE. his load-bearing structural move in mode a.

  • "10x". specific improvement claim.
  • "really" (as triple intensifier)
  • "here's 3 things" / "here's a few examples"

5. content-type modes

mode a: contrarian advice top performer

trigger: common b2b advice is wrong for most of the audience.

structure: specific-anecdote hook → why it's broken → 3-5 numbered alternatives (1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣) → summative principle or bold wager.

top example: "the other day, an agency owner told me..." (465 reactions, 73 comments).

warning: tanks without the numbered payoff. the list is load-bearing.

mode b: event / live session invite

trigger: hosting a mastermind, webinar, live call, product demo.

structure: question + specific credibility numbers (arr, sessions, attendees) → agenda as arrow list → presenter credentials → comment-keyword cta.

example: the 5-phase claude skill invite. "tomorrow at 10am ET, i'm walking ~20 people through the whole thing live."

mode c: product launch

trigger: new databox feature or product enhancement.

structure: value proposition → "you shouldn't need [x]" repetition (4-6 items) → mission tie-back → link + ps wink.

example: databox analytics launch (77R, 16C).

mode d: ai practitioner reflection

trigger: pete trying a new ai tool or changing his stance.

structure: admission → specific moment with a named peer → concrete tactics.

examples: paul roetzer chatgpt post (67R, 41C). the 5-phase claude skill post.

mode e: team / travel

trigger: team offsite, international team visit.

structure: specific place + named people → what he learned → future tease.

example: ptuj slovenia trip (85R, 6C).

mode f: short reaction / satire

trigger: a bad piece of content or sales tactic pete wants to name.

structure: quote the bad thing → savage rewrite → invite reaction.

top example: passive-aggressive cold email rewrite (57R, 24C).

warning: lowest-performing mode when the satire is gentle.

6. do / don't

do

  • name specific people (paul roetzer, april dunford, jeremiah rizzo)
  • include specific numbers early (arr, session count, percentages)
  • lead with a concrete moment or direct quote
  • end with 3-5 numbered alternatives when giving advice
  • include self-deprecation ("i can't take ANY credit", "late adopter")
  • tie back to databox organically, as the practitioner

don't

  • start with philosophical musings or generic openers
  • write opinions without the numbered payoff
  • stack decorative emoji (structural only: 1️⃣ ➡️)
  • use "disruptive", "revolutionary", "game-changing"
  • write abstract advice without a named peer or specific company

## 2. voice archetype

## 3. style fingerprint

### 3a. sentence mechanics
- avg sentence length (measure it, count words across 30+ sentences)
- length variance: consistent / mixed / extreme
- fragment frequency: 0-10 based on % of sentences ≤3 words
- pov: first-person singular / plural / you-voice / mix

### 3b. paragraph rhythm
- avg paragraph length in sentences
- line-break pattern: one-per-line / clustered / mixed
- whitespace density
- visual rhythm (describe it)
include one real 3-4 paragraph block.

### 3c. opening patterns (find 3-5)
each pattern: short label + when used + one verbatim example.

### 3d. closing patterns (find 3-5)

5-10 real connector phrases from the corpus.

step 3. the moments bank prompt

prompt · moments bank 
you are mining a linkedin post archive for specific lived moments that can 
be reused as anchors for future content.

input: the same corpus as part 2.

output: 15-25 entries. each entry is a specific, usable story. not an 
opinion, not a framework.

a moment has: a specific time + a specific place + at least one named 
person or concrete detail + a shift or realization.

NOT moments: abstract claims, advice without a story, product positioning.

scan for posts that open with: "i remember...", "last week...", "on the 
day...", "when i was...", a specific timestamp, or a named person in the 
first 2 sentences.

group by category: career / customer / product / team / personal / 
industry / founding.

entry format:

### <short label>
- **when:** <approximate date or era>
- **category:** <one of the above>
- **setting:** <1 line>

- **details worth keeping:** <bullets with numbers, names, quotes>
- **themes:** <tags>
- **safe to use:** <public | with-edits | consent-needed>
- **source:** <post url or identifier>

hygiene:
- no inventing. every detail from the corpus only.
- thin moments: mark "with-edits" and flag what's missing.
